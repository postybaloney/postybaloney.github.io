Introduction to Neurotech

UC Berkeley, Fall 2023

Course details

  • Description: Introducing our cutting-edge New Member Education Program for Neurotechnology@Berkeley! Dive into the fascinating world of brain-machine interfaces, neural networks, and neuroethics. This comprehensive program equips students with essential knowledge and skills to navigate the dynamic field of neurotechnology. Join us to unlock the potential of the human mind.

  • Requisites: Enroll in our Neurotechnology Education Program with just a passion for learning! No prior experience or specific qualifications are needed. All you require is curiosity and a desire to explore the exciting realms of neurotechnology. Join us and embark on a journey into the future of brain science.

  • Lectures: Wednesday 7-9PM at Evans Hall Room 71

  • Lecturer: Parth Desai, parthrudesai@berkeley.edu.

Grading and policies

  • Attendance: Attendance will be taken in-person so it is imperative that you attend the lectures. If there is a conflict of schedule or any legimitate description as to why you can not attend a lecture, please Slack Parth Desai with the week and reason why you can not attend. You are allowed at maximum 2 excused absences over the course of the semester. These lectures are built to help you learn the content and provide a preview of what to expect in the weeks assignment so it is highly encouraged to attend the lectures.

  • Assignments: Weekly assignments will be posted on the web page, and they are due before the following lecture (Wednesdays from 7-9PM). The assignments will be given and written in the form of Google Colab Python notebooks. The completed assignment should be in the form of a Python Notebook(.ipynb) and submitted online to the assignment submission form. Group discussions about the assignments are highly encouraged, but each student must write his/her own solutions and computer codes and not copy them from anyone else.

  • Project (CANCELLED) : There will be 1 project due by the end of the semester. Further details of your project will be given on the 5th week of NMEP but the general form of the projects is that they will be given and written in the form of Jupyter notebooks. The completed project as well as all previous homework should be uploaded to the assignment submission form as an ipynb-file. Group discussions about the project is required, and only one student from each group should submit the final project but each student should submit all previous homeworks and a note of the group they were in. Late submissions will not be accepted, and might result in a retainment of the club fee.

  • Special arrangements: If you are a student with a disability registered by the Disabled Student Program (DSP) on UCB campus and if you require special arrangements during exams, you must provide the DSP document and make arrangements via email or office hours at least 10 days prior to each exam, explaining your circumstances and what special arrangements need to be done. Also see your GSI as soon as possible to make arrangements for the homework/quizzes.
